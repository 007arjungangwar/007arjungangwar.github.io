About and Implementation Roadmap

This site is being shaped as a personal brand platform for a student and creator working at the intersection of data science, machine learning, deep learning, and scientific computing. The story begins with IIT Palakkad, grows through research interests like Physics-Informed Neural Networks, and expands into content, teaching, and community.

The smartest way to build this is not to launch every feature at once. The stronger approach is to begin with a focused portfolio, then build a content hub around blog posts and videos, and only later add advanced features like digital products, newsletter funnels, and a user dashboard.

The recommendation is to choose a platform based on how you want to work. Microweber is the best all-in-one option for launching quickly, Webstudio is excellent if design freedom matters most, Aether CMS is ideal if you prefer a developer workflow, and AI-based builders are interesting for experiments and rapid prototypes.

Phase 1: Foundation

The first phase should focus on four core pages: Home, About, Projects, and Contact. These pages define your identity, academic background, technical interests, and the value of your work. They also create a clean, professional first impression for visitors coming from LinkedIn, GitHub, or referrals.

The Home page should introduce who you are and direct visitors toward your best work. The About page should tell your story through IIT Palakkad, data science, and your interest in topics like PINNs. The Projects page should feature ML and DL work with GitHub links and short case-study summaries. The Contact page should make it easy to connect through email and social media.

Phase 2: Content Hub

Once the portfolio is stable, the next step is to turn the website into a learning and publishing platform. This phase adds a blog for tutorials, research explainers, and interview-preparation posts. It also brings in YouTube content so your videos and written articles can strengthen each other.

A great early move is to launch one free course, such as Python Basics, to establish teaching credibility and start building a repeat audience. This phase should focus on consistency and clarity rather than complexity.

Phase 3: Monetization and Community

The final phase introduces revenue and audience systems after the foundation is already working. This can include ebooks, templates, interview guides, and other digital products. A newsletter signup becomes valuable here because it helps convert readers into a long-term community.

If there is a real need later, a simple user dashboard can be added for course access or member resources. The key is to add these only after your portfolio and content engine are producing steady value.

Social Media Placement Strategy

LinkedIn should appear prominently on the Contact page, in the footer, and on the About page because it supports your professional identity. GitHub belongs on the Projects page and in the footer so visitors can verify your code and technical depth. Twitter or X works well in blog content and the footer through share-friendly links. Medium can be embedded into the blog section or used for cross-posting, while Instagram fits best on the About page for a more personal layer of your brand.

This strategy keeps the website as the main hub while every external platform supports discovery, authority, and audience growth.
